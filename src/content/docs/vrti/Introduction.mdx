**With VRTI (VR Treadmill Interface) you can control your Bluetooth-enabled treadmill and translate its movement into VRChat.**

VRTI uses [OSC](https://docs.vrchat.com/docs/osc-overview) to turn your real walking pace into movement in VRChat, and can also emulate a virtual Xbox controller through XInput.
You can control your treadmill from the desktop app or from inside VR: start and stop it, adjust speed, use customizable [SteamVR controller bindings](/vrti/getting-started/steamvr-controller-bindings), and enable yaw lock to keep walking straight while you look around.

VRTI also tracks your sessions, including distance, steps, and active time.
It includes long-term statistics, support for syncing sessions to [Strava](https://www.strava.com/), and milestone notifications to help you keep track of your progress over time.

VRTI supports many Bluetooth-enabled treadmills, including various KingSmith WalkingPad models and most treadmills that implement the FTMS (Fitness Machine Service) protocol.
The [supported treadmills](/vrti/supported-devices/treadmills) list is the best place to start, but it is not exhaustive. If your treadmill can be controlled over Bluetooth through its own app, there is a good chance it may work with VRTI too. If you are unsure, try the free demo first to test compatibility.
